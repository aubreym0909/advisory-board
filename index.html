Pricing Strategy · Premium Reposition
Decision (May 20): Move away from competing on price. Reposition as a premium brand. The packaging redesign is the relaunch vehicle — adjust pricing at the same time.

Target: $4.99 retail for snack crackers (from $3.99-$4.00). Customers showed no resistance at $3.99 — go to $4.99.

Benchmarks: Firehook (6oz tub) = $8.99 · Cheez-Its = $4.99/box · Rule: price just below the market leader, not 20% under (too low hurts brand perception).

Structure: Unified price within each brand by size/dimension. All Mariner products same price. All Capicchio products same price. Some will have higher margins, some higher volume — that's fine.

BOM logic: Set price based on BOM + margin of safety. Labor costs (not materials) are the lever to reduce over time via operational efficiency (longer runs, fewer changeovers, SKU consolidation).
Packaging Overhaul · Active
1oz packaging: Defective. Wrong film material — no tear notch, produces crumbs. Phase out ASAP. Use current stock then reset.

New design protocol: Start with Nick (machine compatibility specs + eye-mark alignment) → then Kevin (design specs + templates). Machine-first, not design-first.

Quality control rule: All incoming packaging rolls checked against approved proofs upon receipt. Kevin owns this as a KPI (tracked in Kwan's dashboard). 6-8 week overseas lead time = zero tolerance for errors.

Color consistency: 1oz blue ≠ 4oz blue. Must match exactly in next production run.

New product packaging: Add UPC to new 1oz puff. Add UPC to new snack bag ("fair" brand). New small-bag design needed — determine units per standard shipping case.
